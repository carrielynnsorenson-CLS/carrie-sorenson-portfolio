Profile

About

Educator and consultant with 14+ years of classroom experience and 5+ years of curriculum, assessment, and professional development design. Known for translating language development theory — SFL, WIDA, indigenous language revitalization — into practical tools that actually work for students and communities.

Has served multilingual learners across Title I schools in Minnesota including Somali, Hmong, Karen, Burmese, West African, and Latino students — with consulting work extending to Tlingit, Yugtun, Navajo, Zuni, and Hopi communities in Alaska and New Mexico.

Areas of Practice

Expertise

WIDA Framework & SFL

Seven-year research collaboration with Dr. Cynthia Lundgren contributing to the pedagogical foundation of the WIDA 2020 Framework. Contracted by WIDA in 2015 to develop Key Use Posters.

Indigenous Language Assessment

Co-designed the Lingít X'axaa.áxch (I Speak Tlingit) and Yugtun language assessments — the first of their kind — in partnership with WIDA, Sealaska Heritage Institute, and LKSD.

Curriculum Development

Co-developed K–12 baseline writing framework and two primary ELA units for Navajo, Zuni, and Hopi students with Dr. Cynthia Lundgren — publicly available and in active district use.

Professional Development Design

Designed and delivered 47 SFL-based PD sessions for APS and facilitated ongoing JPA workshops for Tlingit and Yugtun language teachers. Philosophy: lead from behind.

Digital Assessment Infrastructure

Built accessible digital assessment systems using Flexiquiz, Mote, and Google Suite — adapted for remote rural educators with limited tech experience.

Crisis & Trauma-Informed Teaching

Sustained 95%+ student engagement during ICE enforcement crises in Minnesota by rapidly developing a trauma-informed virtual classroom model.
